Warcross

I found Warcross to be a highly intriguing and engaging novel. It challenges us to answer what we value more: our freedom, or our happiness. The story of Emika, a young hacker who finds herself happier than she‚Äôs ever been before surrounded by a world of richness and splendor relates to me like very few other novels I have read. After this novel, I began to consider how anything significant I was working on, or any strong opinions I had would affect other people, and the world. This contemplation has lead me to a few revelations, the clearest one being how often things are a scale rather than a switch. This is easy to say, ‚Äúnot everything is black and white‚Äù, but it is much more difficult to apply this to our lives and spot it in decisions we are making. I have also seen that this balance has made my life much more successful, because it helps me keep my long terms goals in sight without becoming too distracted by one particular goal.
